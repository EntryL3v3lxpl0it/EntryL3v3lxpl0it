# Social Engineering Assessment Methodology

**Document type:** Repeatable assessment playbook for **authorized** social engineering testing
**Scope of applicability:** Phishing, spear-phishing, vishing, smishing, physical pretext, and impersonation assessments conducted with explicit written authorization, to measure and improve an organization's human-layer resilience
**Relationship to the other playbooks:** Social engineering is frequently the **initial-access** vector of a red-team engagement. Credential/payload outcomes that lead to network access hand off to the **Internal/Assumed-Breach** and **AD/Windows-Enterprise** methodologies; harvested-credential and lure-page handling reuse the **Web/API** safe-PoC discipline; every finding enters the **Vulnerability Lifecycle**. This document covers the human-layer assessment itself.
**Standards referenced:** MITRE ATT&CK (Reconnaissance, Initial Access — Phishing T1566 and sub-techniques; T1598 Phishing for Information), NIST SP 800-115, the Social Engineering ethical frameworks (e.g., social-engineer.org code of ethics), and applicable law (see Phase 0). CWE where a finding maps to a technical control gap.
**Toolchain assumed:** This Kali build — `gophish` (campaign framework + metrics), `set`/`setoolkit`, `evilginx2` (AiTM/MFA-phishing *demonstration*), `dnstwist`/`urlcrazy` (lookalike domains), `swaks` (SMTP/spoofing + email-auth checks), and the OSINT suite (`theharvester`, `maltego`, `spiderfoot`, `recon-ng`, `linkedin2username`, `emailharvester`, `h8mail`). `wifiphisher`/`eaphammer` for Wi-Fi pretext where in scope.

> **Operating constraints — these are the methodology, not caveats**
> - **The goal is organizational resilience, not catching individuals.** You measure and improve the human layer and feed awareness/process/technical fixes. You never punish, shame, rank, or expose named employees.
> - **Authorization must come from someone with authority over the targeted people.** IT sign-off is not enough — you need an executive sponsor plus HR and Legal review, because you are testing employees, not systems.
> - **Legal review is mandatory and jurisdiction-specific.** Pretexting laws, recording-consent (one-/two-party), GLBA pretexting prohibitions for financial information, privacy law (GDPR/CCPA) for employee data, and the absolute prohibition on impersonating government, law enforcement, or regulators all gate the engagement.
> - **Duty of care is non-negotiable.** No harmful lures (fake layoffs/terminations, bonuses/raises, deaths/illness, health/disaster/charity exploitation, anything exploiting protected characteristics). Provide an immediate, blame-free teaching moment. Report **aggregate/anonymized** metrics.
> - **Benign by construction.** Lures use markers and tracking only; payloads are benign beacons that prove "would-have-executed," never real malware. Harvested credentials prove capture and are then protected and destroyed — not used beyond the agreed proof (red-team chaining only if RoE explicitly authorizes it).
> - **White cell, deconfliction, and a kill switch** exist before launch. Separate evidence from hypothesis; map to MITRE ATT&CK; minimize and protect all PII.

## 0. Phase 0 — Authorization, Legal & Ethical Scoping (Expanded)

**Goal:** Establish airtight authorization, legal clearance, ethical guardrails, and duty-of-care commitments. This phase is larger than in any technical methodology because the targets are people.

### 0.1 Authorization chain (hard blocker) — `00-scope/`
1. Signed authorization from an **executive sponsor** with authority over the targeted population, **plus documented HR and Legal review/approval**. (For external testers: contract + appropriate insurance + a get-out-of-jail letter naming the engagement.)
2. **Scope — vectors:** which of phishing / spear-phishing / vishing / smishing / physical pretext / impersonation are authorized; explicitly list what is **excluded**.
3. **Scope — population:** which departments/roles; **exclusions** for individuals who must not be targeted (those on leave, in crisis, recently bereaved, or otherwise vulnerable; sometimes execs or whistleblowers). Define how the target list is governed and who may see it.
4. **Announced vs unannounced**, **zero- vs full-knowledge**, and whether this is a standalone SE assessment or the initial-access leg of a red team (defender-testing).
5. **Dates/times/rate**; business-critical periods to avoid; a defined **kill switch** and stop conditions.

### 0.2 Legal clearance (jurisdiction-specific — confirm with counsel)
- **Pretexting law** and any prohibition on obtaining specific information categories by deception (e.g., **GLBA** pretexting for financial data).
- **Recording-consent** rules for vishing (one-party vs two-party/all-party consent states/countries).
- **Privacy law** for handling employee PII and any captured data (GDPR/CCPA等).
- **Hard prohibitions:** never impersonate **government agencies, law enforcement, courts, or regulators**; never impersonate a **real, named third party** (a real bank, a real vendor, a real colleague) without that party's consent; never collect protected-class information.

### 0.3 Ethical guardrails & duty of care
- **Prohibited-pretext list (do not use):** fake terminations/layoffs, fake bonuses/raises/equity, fake death/serious illness of colleagues or family, health-scare/pandemic exploitation, disaster/charity exploitation, anything targeting or exploiting protected characteristics (race, religion, disability, pregnancy, etc.), or anything that causes lasting distress someone "can't unsee."
- **No individual harm:** results are aggregated and anonymized; employees are **not** disciplined, named, ranked, or publicly identified based on the test.
- **Teaching moment:** anyone who interacts gets an immediate, supportive "this was an authorized security test" landing/redirect with brief guidance — never a gotcha.
- **Positive reinforcement:** reporting the lure is the success behavior to encourage and measure.

### 0.4 Deconfliction
- **White cell:** the minimal set of people in the know (sponsor, security lead, sometimes SOC lead) with contacts and a stop procedure.
- **IR deconfliction:** decide whether the SOC/IR is read-in. If unannounced, plan for a real IR response being triggered — and how to stop it from wasting genuine resources or causing third-party reports (e.g., to a real bank/abuse desk).
- **Third-party deconfliction:** notify mailbox/hosting providers as needed; never involve uninvolved third parties.

### 0.5 Decision point
- Missing HR/Legal sign-off, or a vector touches a hard legal prohibition → **STOP** that vector; descope or obtain clearance.
- High-risk population (e.g., clinicians during a crisis) → adjust timing/exclusions or descope.

**Validation gate:** executive + HR + Legal authorization stored; vectors and population scoped with exclusions; legal prohibitions and recording-consent confirmed; prohibited-pretext and duty-of-care commitments documented; white cell, kill switch, and IR deconfliction defined.

## 2. Phase 1 — OSINT & Target Development

**Goal:** Build the org/people picture from **public sources only**, with PII minimized. MITRE ATT&CK: T1589/T1591/T1598 (Phishing for Information).

```bash
theHarvester -d client.tld -b bing,duckduckgo,crtsh -f 01-osint/harvester
spiderfoot -s client.tld -o csv > 01-osint/spiderfoot.csv          # within RoE
linkedin2username -c "Client" -o 01-osint/usernames.txt            # username modeling
# Email format (hunter.io), org structure, tech stack (login portals: owa/sso/adfs),
# vendors/processes that make a plausible, BENIGN pretext.
```

### 2.1 Governance & minimization
- Collect only what the campaign needs (name, role, work email, department). **Do not** assemble intrusive personal dossiers, surveil personal social media, or collect protected-class data.
- Breached-credential references: note exposure as an *organizational* finding (e.g., "N corporate emails appear in public breach sets") — do **not** test or reuse real employee passwords. 
- Target list lives encrypted in `99-secure-store/`; access limited to the test team.

**Validation gate:** public-source intel gathered; PII-minimized target list governed and stored encrypted; pretext seeds identified that are plausible **and** benign.

## 3. Phase 2 — Campaign Design & Pretext Development

**Goal:** Design lures and difficulty that produce useful resilience data, within the guardrails. *Pretext development here is principled and guardrailed — not a manipulation script targeting named individuals.*

### 3.1 Vector & difficulty calibration
- Choose vector(s) per scope. Calibrate difficulty to the program's maturity: a first assessment uses an obvious-to-moderate lure (baseline); mature programs justify more realistic spear-phishing. State the difficulty so metrics are interpretable.
- Define the **success behaviors** you measure (report the lure = good; click/submit/execute = teachable) — framed to reward reporting.

### 3.2 Pretext principles (the legitimate influence levers, used benignly)
Real-world adversaries exploit authority, urgency, familiarity/trust, helpfulness, and curiosity. An authorized assessment may emulate these **only** through benign business scenarios (e.g., a generic IT/SSO notice, a routine internal-process message). Apply the guardrails: no harmful themes (0.3), no real-person/real-brand/government impersonation, no protected-class targeting, nothing that causes lasting distress.

### 3.3 Lure & landing design
- Lure (email/SMS/call outline) and **landing page** built on infrastructure you control. The landing page captures only the interaction needed for metrics and immediately presents the **teaching moment**.
- For credential-capture campaigns: capture *that* a credential was submitted (for the metric) while minimizing storage of the actual secret — prove capture, don't hoard plaintext.
- For payload campaigns: a **benign beacon/marker** that records "would-have-executed" (e.g., a tracked document open / macro-enabled benign callback) — never real malware.

### 3.4 Approval gate
Every lure, landing page, and pretext is reviewed against the prohibited-pretext list and (where required) re-confirmed with the sponsor/Legal before build.

**Validation gate:** vectors + difficulty + success metrics defined; pretexts pass the guardrail review; lures/landing pages approved; capture minimized by design.

## 4. Phase 3 — Infrastructure Setup

**Goal:** Stand up isolated, controlled campaign infrastructure. All on assets you own.

```bash
# Lookalike / category domains you register and control
dnstwist client.tld | tee 03-infra/dnstwist.txt          # candidate domains (you register them)
urlcrazy client.tld | tee 03-infra/urlcrazy.txt
# Campaign framework + metrics
#   gophish: groups (targets), email templates, landing pages, sending profile, tracking
gophish    # configure via its admin UI/API; TLS on landing pages
# Email-auth posture of the TARGET (defensive finding) — and your own deliverability
swaks --to test@client.tld --from notice@yourinfra.tld --server <mx>   # observe SPF/DKIM/DMARC handling
# MFA-phishing DEMONSTRATION (only if RoE authorizes testing MFA resistance)
#   evilginx2 as an AiTM proxy to demonstrate that non-phishing-resistant MFA can be relayed
```

### 4.1 Infrastructure hygiene
- Dedicated domains/sending infra, TLS landing pages, tracking that ties interactions to the campaign (not to durable personal profiles).
- Note the **target's** email-authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) and link-rewriting/sandboxing as **defensive findings** (these are the technical controls that should stop you).
- Isolate campaign infra from production and from any real client systems.

**Validation gate:** controlled domains/sending infra + TLS landing pages live; metrics/tracking working; target email-auth posture recorded; MFA-demo infra only if RoE-authorized.

## 5. Phase 4 — Pre-Launch Validation & Deconfliction

**Goal:** Verify everything is safe, legal, and won't cause collateral damage before a single real target is contacted.

1. **Seed test:** send to test mailboxes / the white cell; confirm rendering, links, landing page, teaching-moment redirect, and metrics capture.
2. **Sample review:** sponsor/Legal review of the final lure + landing + (if vishing) call outline, against the guardrails.
3. **White-cell brief:** confirm who's read-in, the kill switch, and the stop procedure; confirm IR deconfliction decision.
4. **Allow-list/deliverability** coordination if announced; ensure the campaign won't trigger third-party abuse reports.
5. **Final go/no-go** with documented sign-off.

**Validation gate:** seed test clean; final sample approved; white cell briefed; kill switch verified; documented go/no-go recorded.

---

## 6. Phase 5 — Execution

**Goal:** Run the campaign under control, monitoring continuously with safety overrides. MITRE ATT&CK: T1566 (Phishing).

- **Controlled launch:** stagger sends/rate per RoE and timing window; avoid business-critical periods.
- **Live monitoring:** watch delivery, interactions, and any **distress signals** (confused/anxious replies, real IR escalation, people contacting the impersonated function). 
- **Responder handling:** people who reply or call get a prompt, supportive resolution; people who **report** the lure are recorded as the success behavior (and not "tipped off" in a way that taints metrics unfairly — handle per plan).
- **Kill switch:** stop immediately on stop conditions (real harm, unintended scope, a real incident colliding with the test, Legal/sponsor instruction). Log the event.

**Validation gate:** campaign executed within window/rate; distress signals handled; any kill-switch events logged; no out-of-scope contact.

---

## 7. Phase 6 — Measurement & Metrics

**Goal:** Produce resilience metrics that drive improvement — **aggregated and anonymized**, never an individual scoreboard.

Core metrics (segment by department/role/difficulty, **not** by named person):
- Delivery rate (how many landed = email-auth/filtering effectiveness).
- Open rate, **click rate**, **credential-submit rate**, **payload-"execution" (benign beacon) rate**.
- **Report rate** and **time-to-report** (the key resilience indicator).
- Dwell time; repeat vs first-time interaction at the cohort level.
- For MFA-demo (if authorized): whether non-phishing-resistant MFA could be relayed (a *control* finding, not a person finding).

> Frame everything as control effectiveness: a high click rate is a *training/process* signal; a high delivery rate is an *email-authentication/filtering* signal; a high report rate is a *positive culture* signal.

**Validation gate:** metrics computed at cohort level; no individual is identified, ranked, or singled out; technical-control signals (delivery, MFA) separated from human-behavior signals.

## 8. Phase 7 — Vishing / Smishing / Physical *(if in scope)*

**Goal:** Execute non-email vectors with their distinct legal and safety rails.

### 8.1 Vishing / smishing
- **Recording consent** per jurisdiction (one-/two-party) confirmed before any call recording.
- **Caller identity:** never impersonate law enforcement/government/regulators or a real named third party. Use a benign, generic internal/vendor-style pretext within scope.
- Provide a graceful exit and teaching moment; handle any distress immediately; respect "no."

### 8.2 Physical pretext
- Tailgating, badge/visitor pretext, USB drops, reception social engineering — only with explicit physical-scope authorization, site/safety briefing, and a **physical get-out-of-jail letter carried on-person**.
- **Safety first:** never breach genuine safety controls, never put staff or yourself at physical risk, never enter restricted-for-safety areas; comply immediately if challenged and present authorization.
- USB drops use **benign beacons** that record "would-have-executed," not malware.

**Validation gate:** consent/legal rails satisfied per vector; physical authorization carried; safety preserved; distress/refusal respected; benign markers only.

---

## 9. Phase 8 — Awareness & Teaching Moment

**Goal:** Turn the engagement into improvement, supportively.
- Anyone who interacted has already received the immediate, blame-free teaching-moment redirect (Phase 2/5). Now provide/coordinate **brief targeted micro-training** tied to the specific lure cues they missed.
- Reinforce and thank **reporters**; publicize (anonymously) that reporting worked.
- Hand the organization reusable awareness content and the cues that distinguished this lure.

**Validation gate:** teaching content delivered/handed off; reporting reinforced; no punitive messaging.

---

## 10. Phase 9 — Reporting

**Goal:** Deliver aggregate findings, root causes, and prioritized controls — protecting individuals throughout. Readable by a PM/exec, defensible to security engineers.

### 10.1 Structure
1. Executive summary (resilience posture: technical controls vs human behavior vs reporting culture; key risks).
2. Scope, authorization (exec+HR+Legal), vectors, population (described, not named), difficulty, RoE, deconfliction.
3. Aggregate metrics (Phase 6), by cohort.
4. Findings (ranked), template below.
5. Root-cause analysis: **technical** (email auth, filtering, MFA resistance, URL handling), **process** (no reporting channel, slow IR), **awareness** (specific missed cues).
6. Prioritized recommendations (DMARC enforcement, phishing-resistant MFA/FIDO2, report-phish button + fast triage, targeted training, link rewriting/sandboxing).
7. What the defenders did well/poorly (if red-team/defender-testing) with ATT&CK mapping.

### 10.2 Finding template
```markdown
### [Finding ID] <Title>
**Summary:** <plain-language resilience risk for an exec>
**Vector / surface:** <phishing/vishing/smishing/physical; control involved>
**Weakness classification:** <technical control gap / process gap / awareness gap>
**MITRE ATT&CK:** <T1566(.xxx) / T1598 / etc.>     **CWE:** CWE-### (if technical; uncertain?)
**Affected cohort:** <department/role — NEVER a named individual>
**Evidence (aggregate):** <delivery/click/submit/report rates; control behavior; screenshots of lure/landing — no PII>
**Root cause:** <which control failed: email auth, MFA resistance, no reporting path, missed cue class>
**Impact:** <evidence-bounded; what an attacker could achieve via this gap; CONFIRMED vs INFERRED>
**Recommendation:** <specific control: DMARC reject, FIDO2 MFA, report button + triage SLA, targeted training>
**Validation after fix:** <re-test metric that should improve; the control that should now block>
**Residual risk:** <what remains; human factor is never zero>
```

### 10.3 Quality checks
No named individuals; metrics aggregated; technical vs human vs cultural causes separated; recommendations actionable; ATT&CK/CWE supported or marked uncertain; duty-of-care affirmed.

**Validation gate:** report uses aggregate/anonymized data only; findings map to controls; individuals protected; recommendations prioritized.

---

## 11. Phase 10 — Data Handling & Cleanup

**Goal:** Protect and dispose of sensitive data and decommission infrastructure.
1. **Destroy** harvested credentials and captured PII per the retention agreement; log the destruction (`10-data-handling/`).
2. **Decommission** campaign domains/infra/landing pages; revoke any access.
3. **Secure-store** retains only the minimal evidence needed for the report, encrypted, with a destruction date.
4. Provide the white cell a timeline for deconfliction and confirm any triggered real IR is closed out.

**Validation gate:** captured credentials/PII destroyed and logged; infra decommissioned; only minimal encrypted evidence retained with a destruction date.

*This methodology is for authorized human-layer resilience testing only. Authorization runs through the executive sponsor plus HR and Legal; legal review and duty-of-care are load-bearing, not optional. Emulate adversary influence only through benign, guardrailed scenarios; use benign markers and minimize captured data; deliver a blame-free teaching moment; and report aggregate, anonymized results that drive technical, process, and awareness improvements. Protect the people you test — the objective is a stronger organization, never a list of who failed.*
